Architecture Through Ages

Ancient Architecture

Ancient architecture, particularly in Egypt, was deeply rooted in religious beliefs and the concept of eternity. It is famous for its monumental scale, using massive stone blocks and precise mathematical proportions. The structures, such as the Great Pyramids and massive temples, were designed to last forever, showcasing incredible engineering skills that remain a mystery today.

Islamic Architecture

Islamic architecture introduced a new level of spiritual artistry and decorative detail. It is globally recognized for its iconic domes, pointed arches, and towering minarets. The use of complex geometric patterns, known as Arabesque, and beautiful calligraphy reflects a balance between physical structure and spiritual beauty, with a focus on interior courtyards and natural light.
